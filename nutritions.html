Whey, Honey And Peanut Butter Protein Bar Recipe

Nutrition Facts

Amount Per Serving: 120

Calories: 526

Protein: 31.2g

Carbs: 37g

Fat: 31g

A simple-to-make (and healthy) protein bar that requires only 4 ingredients: chocolate whey protein power, peanut butter, honey and oats.

Ingredients:

  • Whey Protein Powder, Chocolate, 6 scoops
  • Oats, 1 cup
  • Peanut Butter 16 ounces
  • Honey, 8 tablespoons

    Cooking Instructions:

  • This bar is a great choice for muscle building and bulking, and can be used in between meals as a quick and easy high protein snack.
  • Combine the honey and peanut butter in a microwave safe bowl. Place this combination into a microwave and heat for 20 seconds.
  • Stir and heat for an additional 20 seconds. Continue this pattern until the honey and peanut butter are soft enough to be mixed with the oats and whey.
  • Next, mix in the whey protein powder and oats. Spread this mixture into a 9x9 or 9x13 pan. You may want to lightly grease the pan, or line the bottom and sides with baking parchment paper.
  • Place the pan in your fridge for an hour. Remove, cut into desired squares, and enjoy!

Chocolate And Peanut Butter Protein Bars Recipe

Nutrition Facts

Amount Per Serving: 1

Calories: 374

Protein: 24g

Carbs: 42g

Fat: 8g

These homemade protein bars taste amazing, almost like a brownie without the guilt. Main ingredients are chocolate whey protein and peanut butter.

Ingredients:

  • 2 scoops Chocolate Whey Protein Powder
  • 3 tbsp Peanut Butter, Smooth
  • 2 cups Oats
  • 5 Egg Whites
  • 3 Bananas, Medium
  • 100g Milk
  • 2tbsp Honey
  • 1.5 tsp Cinnamon

    Cooking Instructions:

  • Preheat your oven to 350 degrees F.
  • Now place the oats in a mixing bowl and add in the cinnamon and chocolate whey protein powder
  • Next add in the peanut butter and mix thoroughly
  • Mash your bananas and add them to this mixture, along with the honey and egg whites. Mix well. Finally, add the milk and mix again.
  • Pour batter into a 9x9 or 9x13 greased baking pan. You may also use a pan lined with parchment paper.
  • Bake for 15 to 20 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean from the center of the pan. Allow the bars to cool and then cut into 6 to 8 bars.

Crustless Low-Carb Pumpkin Pie Recipe

Nutrition Facts

Amount Per Serving: 1

Calories: 163

Protein: 23g

Carbs: 11g

Fat: 3g

Enjoying dessert, while staying on track with your meal plan was never so easy and delicious! This recipe is simple, packed with protein and the perfect way to finish off your Thanksgiving meal!

Ingredients:

  • 2 Large Eggs
  • 1 ½ cup Nonfat Plain Greek Yogurt
  • (15oz) Pure Pumpkin (not pie filling)*
  • 2 tbsp. Pumpkin Pie Spice
  • Sweetener, optional
  • Maple Syrup and Whipped Topping, optional

    Cooking Instructions:

  • Preheat oven to 325 degrees
  • Spray a pie dish with nonstick spray
  • Add all ingredients of a bowl and mix well
  • Place an oven safe dish full of water on rack below where pie will go (this prevents cracking on the top of the pie)
  • Pour mixture into dish and bake for ~45-50 minutes or until just set
  • Mixture will seem soft at first. Place in refrigerator to set for 4-6 hours or preferably, overnight.
  • Top with a drizzle of fresh maple syrup or whipped cream, if desired.

Mini Coconut Chocolate Chip Protein Cookies Recipe

Nutrition Facts

Amount Per Serving: 1

Calories: 60

Protein: 2.5g

Carbs: 3.6g

Fat: 4.2g

Get your coconut fix with these protein-filled mini cookies that combine chocolate and coconut so deliciously you'll forget they're good for you as well!

Ingredients:

  • (60g) Coconut Flour
  • 30g Oat Flout
  • 1 tsp. Baking Soda
  • 3 large Egg Whites
  • ¼ cup Full-Fat Coconut Milk*
  • 3 tbsp. Coconut Oil, melted
  • 1 tsp. Pure Vanilla Extract
  • ¼ cup Shredded Coconut
  • ¼ cup Chocolate Chips
  • Chocolate Peanut Butter

    Cooking Instructions:

  • Preheat oven to 325 degrees
  • Line a baking sheet with parchment paper

  • Bake for ~12 minutes or until slightly golden (will still feel soft)

  • Frost with chocolate peanut butter, if desired
  • Enjoy!

Carbs: 50g

Treat yourself to something sweet utilizing this recipe for healthy apple turnovers. Pair it with your favorite protein for the perfect post workout snack!

Ingredients:

  • 2 apples, cored and thinly sliced
  • 1 tbs coconut oil (or butter)
  • ¼ to ½ cup xylitol (or coconut sugar)*
  • ½ tbs ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbs cornstarch
  • ½ package frozen puff pastery sheets, thawed
  • ¼ cup egg whites
  • toppings, optional

    Cooking Instructions:

  • Preheat oven to 400 degrees F
  • Combine xylitol, cinnamon and cornstarch in a bowl. Set aside.
  • Melt coconut oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add in apples and mix well.
  • Allow to cook for 3-4 minutes. Add dry ingredients into apple mixture and mix until all apples are coated.
  • Allow to cook until sauce thickens.
  • Remove from heat and set aside.
  • Unfold pastry sheets and cut into four squares.
  • Spoon apples into center of each pastry sheet. Fold over from corner to corner, creating a triangle shape.
  • Press edges down with a fork to seal in apples.
  • Place on a parchment lined baking sheet.
  • Place on a parchment lined baking sheet.
  • Bake for about 20 minutes or until golden.
  • Allow to cool slightly before enjoying!

Pulled BBQ Chicken Potato Skins Recipe

Nutrition Facts

Amount Per Serving: 1

Calories: 327

Protein: 38g

Carbs: 28g

Fat: 7g

Need a snack for game time? Love finger food? Give this BBQ chicken potato skin recipe a try for a high protein spin on the delicious potato skin!

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs small dutch potatoes
  • ½ kg (raw weight) chicken breast
  • ½ cup favorite BBQ sauce
  • 1 cup reduced fat shredded cheddar cheese

Cooking Instructions:

  • Preheat oven to 425 degrees
  • Line a baking sheet with foil
  • Rinse potatoes and pierce with a fork
  • Place potatoes on a baking sheet and bake for about 1 hour or until tender
  • While potatoes are cooking, boil chicken for about 8-10 minutes or until cooked through
  • When chicken is done, shred it by using 2 forks
  • Toss the chicken with the BBQ sauce and set aside
  • When potatoes are done, remove from the oven and allow to cool enough to handle
  • Cut the potatoes in half and scoop out the middle, leaving a small bit of potato along the sides
  • Evenly divide the pulled BBQ chicken among the potatoes
  • Sprinkle with shredded cheddar cheese
  • Return to the oven and bake an additional 20 minutes or until cheese is melted
  • Finish off with extra BBQ sauce, if desired
  • Enjoy!
